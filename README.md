# Mackenzie Alpha Sniper

A structured framework for analyzing stocks through their quarterly earnings and making clear-eyed investment decisions. Inspired by McKinsey's approach to structured problem-solving -- hypothesis-driven, evidence-based, and designed to separate signal from noise.

## What This Is

The McKinsey playbook is built around breaking big problems into smaller, testable pieces and pressure-testing every assumption before committing to a conclusion. Alpha Sniper applies that same discipline to stock analysis. Instead of vibes-based conviction or blindly following price targets, you build a structured **Configuration Card** for each stock -- a single, self-contained document that captures the thesis, the numbers, the risks, and the pre-committed exit plan.

The card gets refreshed every quarter. No rebuilding from scratch -- just updating what changed.

## The Three Phases

### Phase 1 -- Foundation & Business Model

This is where you figure out what you actually own. Before touching a single financial table, you nail down:

- **Identity & classification** -- what kind of business is this? SaaS, hardware, fintech? The classification drives thresholds for the entire analysis.
- **Thesis** -- a falsifiable statement. Not "great company with strong moat." Something specific enough that one bad quarter could challenge it.
- **Business model & products** -- how does the company create and capture value, product by product.
- **Revenue streams** -- where the money comes from, broken out by segment with quarterly trends.
- **Competitive landscape** -- named competitors with real threat assessments, not vague hand-waving. Plus an honest moat evaluation, including what the moat *doesn't* protect.
- **Management assessment** -- guidance accuracy tracked with actual numbers, sandbagging factors calculated from data, and credibility ratings for each executive.
- **Cross-quarter trends** -- a 5-quarter acceleration table showing whether things are getting better or worse.

### Phase 2 -- Financials & Forward Assessment

This is the number-crunching phase. You pull 5 quarters of data and build:

- **Income statement progression** -- revenue, segments, margins, EPS across 5 quarters.
- **Balance sheet progression** -- liquidity, debt, receivables, dilution trends.
- **Earnings quality forensics** -- FCF conversion, stock-based comp, DSO, ROIC. Each quarter gets a single verdict: Clean, Minor concerns, Investigate, or Red flag.
- **Thesis-weighted scorecard** -- metrics scored and weighted based on your thesis. The key metric from Phase 1 gets 2x weight. Weights are locked -- you don't get to change them to make a deteriorating number look better.
- **Sector-specific metrics** -- 3-4 metrics that matter specifically for this company's business model.
- **Catalyst pipeline** -- upcoming events that could move the stock, with impact ratings.
- **Sell-side landscape** -- not just price targets, but which analysts have the best frameworks and where the Street has blind spots.
- **Management credibility update** -- did they deliver on last quarter's promises? Credibility-adjusted forward expectations.

### Phase 3 -- Risk, Stress Test & Assembly

The adversarial phase. You actively try to break your own thesis before locking the card.

- **Kill criteria** -- pre-committed exit triggers with specific thresholds. Written before you need them so emotions don't cloud the decision. Each one is tested for false positives.
- **Valuation framework** -- reverse DCF to see what growth the market is pricing in, bull/base/bear scenarios with probability-weighted expected value.
- **Quarterly baseline** -- a snapshot of where things stand right now, plus a pre-earnings checklist for the next report. What does the market need to hear? What are your specific thresholds?
- **Risk inventory** -- every material risk in one place with severity, probability, and leading indicators to watch. Includes insider activity analysis.

The final assembled card runs about 1,600 lines and passes a 14-point consistency audit before it ships.

## Quarterly Refresh

Not everything gets updated every quarter. The framework has a clear routing:

- **Every quarter**: financials, scorecard, sector metrics, management credibility, baseline snapshot
- **On material change**: competitive landscape, catalysts, sell-side, risk inventory
- **Annually or on thesis change**: foundation sections, management profiles, kill criteria, valuation

This keeps the workload manageable while making sure nothing goes stale.
