Model Type
Capital-light vertically integrated NAND flash — fab partnership JV model (50/50 Kioxia manufacturing) with in-house controller design, firmware, and system integration

Normal Operating Profile

Sandisk is a pure-play NAND flash storage company that designs controllers and firmware in-house while sourcing substantially all flash memory wafers from Flash Ventures, a 50/50 joint venture with Kioxia Corporation operating eight manufacturing facilities in Japan (six Yokkaichi, two Kitakami).

The JV model results in capital intensity of ~5-9% capex-to-revenue vs. 30%+ for vertically integrated competitors (Micron, Samsung), producing structurally superior FCF conversion when margins normalize. Gross margins are highly cyclical, ranging from 7% (FY2023 trough) to 51% (Q2 FY2026 peak) with normalized non-GAAP range of 30-45% through a full cycle.

The NAND industry is characterized by 2-3 year boom-bust cycles driven by supply/demand imbalance, technology node transitions, and hyperscaler capital spending patterns. SNDK has no DRAM business — pure NAND exposure.

Thesis & Key Metric

annual / thesis change

Falsifiable Thesis Statement (card author analysis)

Sandisk is transitioning from a cyclical commodity NAND supplier to a structural growth story driven by AI datacenter storage demand, with the capital-light Kioxia JV model generating superior FCF vs. vertically integrated competitors.

The thesis requires: (1) Enterprise SSD revenue growing to 25%+ of total revenue by FY2027 (from ~15% in Q2 FY2026), (2) non-GAAP gross margins sustaining above 40% through FY2027, and (3) NAND supply/demand remaining undersupplied through calendar year 2027.

Falsification trigger: Datacenter revenue YoY growth <10% for 2 consecutive quarters, or non-GAAP GM <30% for 2 consecutive quarters would directly challenge the thesis.
Date written: March 10, 2026  ·  Last reviewed: March 10, 2026

3-Sentence Bear Case (card author analysis, drawing on Citron short thesis and historical Samsung behavior)

Samsung's latent NAND capacity (bear estimates suggest multiples of prior peak) threatens demand sustainability — once HBM yields normalize, Samsung has historically responded by increasing NAND supply to pressure competitor margins, which could collapse SNDK's gross margins back to 15-25%. YMTC's expanding capacity provides additional low-cost supply in non-U.S. markets, eroding ASPs globally. The capital-light JV model becomes a constraint in downcycles because Sandisk cannot independently control capacity utilization — committed JV payments ($1.17B CY2026-2029 per 10-K) represent fixed costs that compress margins when pricing deteriorates.
