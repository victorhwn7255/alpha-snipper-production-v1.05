S2Thesis & Key Metricannual / thesis change
Falsifiable Thesis Statement

Lumentum's leadership position in InP EML laser chips — the photonic bottleneck inside high-speed optical modules — combined with vertical integration spanning components to systems (transceivers, OCS, CPO lasers), supports the case for 50%+ YoY revenue growth through FY2027 as AI demand drives quarterly revenue from $665M toward management's $2B/quarter target within roughly 18-24 months, with non-GAAP operating margins expanding from 25% toward 40%.

Falsification triggers: Revenue YoY <40% for 2Q | Non-GAAP OM declining QoQ for 2Q AND <25% | EML share <40% | Liquidity <$500M AND Debt/EBITDA >8×
3-Sentence Bear Case

Competition from silicon photonics and Coherent's 6-inch InP line could erode EML demand and compress margins, while any hyperscaler capex slowdown would reduce backlog visibility and leave customer concentration elevated (Q2 FY26 top-2 customers represented 24% and 17% of revenue). NVIDIA's March 2026 nonexclusive strategic agreement and $2B investment strengthen demand visibility but still leave architecture and customer concentration risk if any major customer shifts design choices or internalizes lasers. $3.2B in convertible debt and 27.4% YoY diluted-share growth amplify financial risk while BIS/DOJ subpoenas add operational uncertainty.

S3Business Model & Product Architectureannual / thesis change
1. EML Laser Chips (100G/200G InP)
CoreLeadership position

Photonic engine in high-speed optical modules. 8 EMLs per 800G module; 512 per 51.2 Tbps switch. 200G EMLs carry roughly 2× the ASP of 100G parts. Management commentary continues to point to tight supply and strong visibility through CY2027. Vertical integration runs from InP epitaxy to die packaging.

2. Cloud Transceivers (800G/1.6T — CloudLight)
GrowthScale

Complete 800G/1.6T OSFP/QSFP-DD modules. CloudLight acquisition ($750M, Nov 2023) expanded SAM 5×. 1.6T margins "significantly better than 800G." Revenue growing beyond initial $1B annual cap. CW laser vertical integration planned late Q2/early Q3 FY26.

3. Optical Circuit Switches (R300 MEMS)
Growth / Emerging

Replaces electrical spine switches. 3D MEMS beam-steering enables any-to-any optical paths in milliseconds with materially lower power than comparable electrical fabrics. At OFC 2026, management said OCS backlog exceeded $400M, the first $10M quarter arrived about three months early, and a new multi-billion-dollar third OCS order had been secured.

4. Co-Packaged Optics / UHP Lasers
EmergingNVIDIA strategic partner

Ultra-high-power InP lasers for CPO. NVIDIA's March 2026 agreement with Lumentum is explicitly nonexclusive, but it includes a multibillion purchase commitment, future capacity access rights, and a $2B strategic investment. At OFC 2026, management highlighted 800+ mW performance exceeding 1.0 W at 25°C and framed CPO as a 2027+ commercialization vector.

5. Telecom & DCI Components
Core (Legacy)

Tunable lasers, coherent components, pump lasers, ROADMs. Narrow-linewidth assemblies grew 8 consecutive quarters. Pump lasers +90% YoY in Q2. Stable cash flow but lower growth than cloud/AI.

6. Industrial Tech
Legacy / Optionality

Fiber lasers, 3D sensing VCSELs, ultrafast lasers. 3D sensing grew modestly on new smartphone launch. PicoBlade Compact design win funnel expanding. Not thesis-critical but provides diversification.
