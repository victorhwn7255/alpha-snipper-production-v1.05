Normal Operating Profile

Tesla operates as a vertically integrated automotive and energy manufacturer with direct-to-consumer sales (no dealer network), in-house battery cell and powertrain production, and proprietary software (FSD, Autobidder, Powerhub). Two reportable segments: Automotive and Energy Generation & Storage. FY2025 revenue mix: Automotive ~73%, Energy ~13.5%, Services & Other ~13.2%.

Normalized gross margins are 16-20% consolidated. Auto GM ex-credits range 12-18%. Energy segment margins are higher and expanding. FCF conversion is low for current investment phase — FY2024 was $3.6B, FY2025 recovered to $6.2B. With FY2026 CapEx guided >$20B, FCF will likely turn negative.

Thesis & Key Metric

annual / thesis change

Falsifiable Thesis Statement

Tesla's transition from a volume automotive manufacturer to an AI/autonomy platform will be validated by (1) Robotaxi scaling beyond Austin to 10+ US markets by end of 2026, generating measurable ride-hailing revenue, (2) automotive gross margins ex-credits sustaining above 15% despite Model S/X discontinuation, and (3) the $20B+ CapEx cycle funded without dilutive equity raises, with energy storage growing to >15% of total revenue by Q4 2026.

Falsification triggers: Auto GM ex-credits <13% for 2 consecutive quarters | Robotaxi <5 US markets by Dec 2026 | Liquidity <$20B or dilutive raise >$5B
Date written: March 3, 2026  ·  Last reviewed: March 3, 2026

3-Sentence Bear Case

The $20B+ CapEx program overwhelms Tesla's $44B cash position without generating near-term returns, forcing dilutive capital raises or project cancellations. Core automotive margins compress as Chinese competitors (BYD, NIO, Xpeng) accelerate global expansion, US EV incentives phase out, and Musk's political activities damage brand perception in key markets. Robotaxi scaling stalls on regulatory fragmentation while Waymo extends its operational lead, and Optimus remains pre-revenue through 2027, making Tesla's trillion-dollar+ valuation increasingly dependent on optionality that never converts to cash flow.

Business Model & Product Architecture

annual / thesis change
1. Model Y / Model 3 (mass-market EVs)
CoreScale~70% of Rev

Model Y is bestselling car of any kind globally (~1.1M unit/yr run rate). Refreshed across all 4 factories in Q1 2025. ASP range $35-55K. Auto GM ex-credits 12.5-17.9% over trailing 5Q.

2. Cybertruck (electric pickup)
Growth / Emerging

Full-size electric pickup. Premium ASP ~$80-100K. Ramping at Gigafactory Texas. Likely margin-dilutive in 2025. Included in "Other Models" count (50,850 units FY2025 across S/X/Cybertruck/Semi).

3. Energy Generation & Storage
Core / Growth~13.5% of Rev

Megapack (grid-scale) and Powerwall (residential). FY2025: $12.8B (+27% YoY), 46.7 GWh deployed (record). Higher margins than automotive. Autobidder and Powerhub software create recurring revenue. Tariff headwinds ~$200M/quarter.

4. Services & Other
Cash Flow Stabilizer~13.2% of Rev

Supercharging (open to non-Tesla, NACS standard), insurance (13 states), parts, used vehicles. FY2025: $12.5B (+19% YoY). Services margin declined to 8.8% in Q4 from service center hiring ahead of fleet growth.

5. Robotaxi / Autonomous Ride-Hailing
Pre-revenue (launched Jun 2025)

Operating Model Y fleet in Austin and Bay Area. Cybercab purpose-built for 50-60 hrs/week duty cycle. Per-mile cost structure not yet disclosed. Currently in 2 US markets.

6. Optimus (humanoid robot)
Pre-revenue

General-purpose autonomous humanoid robot. Gen 3 unveil planned for Q1 2026 (per Q4/FY25 update). First production line being installed; SOP targeted before end of 2026 with eventual planned capacity of 1M units/year. Leverages FSD neural network and in-house AI chip design.
